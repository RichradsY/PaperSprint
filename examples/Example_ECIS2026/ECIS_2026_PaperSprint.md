### Seed Title Set

1. Designing Enterprise GenAI Copilots for Productivity Without Eroding Worker Dignity
2. When AI Assistance Becomes Algorithmic Pressure: A Socio-Technical View of Enterprise Copilots
3. Productive, Accountable, Human: Re-imagining GenAI Copilot Governance in Knowledge Work

### Seed Abstract

Organizations are rapidly deploying generative AI copilots to accelerate knowledge work, improve decision support, and standardize output quality. Yet the same systems may also intensify monitoring, narrow professional judgment, and shift accountability onto workers when AI-generated outputs are wrong, opaque, or difficult to challenge. This paper proposes a socio-technical account of how copilot design and governance choices shape both productivity outcomes and worker dignity. We argue that transparency, overrideability, monitoring intensity, and task criticality jointly determine whether AI assistance is experienced as empowerment or algorithmic pressure. The paper contributes by moving beyond simple adoption or performance narratives toward a human-centered view of enterprise AI governance, clarifying boundary conditions under which productivity gains are compatible with responsible digital technology use. The study can be advanced either as a conceptual framework, a comparative case study, or a mixed-method design around organizational deployments of GenAI copilots.

### Draft Research Question

How do enterprise GenAI copilot design and governance choices shape the balance between productivity gains and worker dignity in knowledge work?

### Draft Contribution Logic

- Theoretical contribution: links enterprise AI governance to a human-centered outcome that is usually mentioned normatively but rarely theorized precisely in operational IS terms
- Empirical contribution path: identifies testable or observable design and governance dimensions such as transparency, overrideability, monitoring intensity, and task criticality
- Practical contribution: gives organizations a language for governing AI copilots beyond raw adoption and performance metrics

### Feasible Method Paths

- Conceptual paper path: build a governance framework around dignity, autonomy, accountability, and task conditions
- Qualitative path: comparative case study across 2-4 organizations or work groups using GenAI copilots
- Mixed-method path: interview-based construct development followed by survey or vignette validation
- TREO path: submit the conceptual core plus an empirical roadmap as a 3-page extended abstract

### Fatal Risks

- "Worker dignity" may remain rhetorical unless defined in observable terms
- The paper could collapse into generic AI ethics unless anchored in a concrete organizational context
- A pure commentary tone would be punished by ECIS reviewers unless the contribution is sharpened into IS theory, mechanism, and boundary conditions

### Immediate Hardening Moves

- Define the unit of analysis: worker experience, team-level governance, or organizational deployment regime
- Choose one focal context: consulting, software engineering, customer service, marketing, higher education, or public administration
- Decide whether dignity is the main outcome or whether the paper should instead foreground autonomy, accountability, or quality of judgment
