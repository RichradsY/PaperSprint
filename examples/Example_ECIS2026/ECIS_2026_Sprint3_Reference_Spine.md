# ECIS 2026 Sprint 3 Reference Spine

Updated: 2026-03-10 23:40:00 CET

## Purpose

This spine provides verified, non-fabricated core references for the TREO draft. Every entry below was checked against DOI metadata before inclusion.

## Core Sources

### 1. Affordances

Leonardi, P. M. (2011). When flexible routines meet flexible technologies: Affordance, constraint, and the imbrication of human and material agencies. *MIS Quarterly, 35*(1), 147-167. https://doi.org/10.2307/23043493

- Why it matters: strongest anchor for the paper's affordance logic
- Use it for: explaining why the same GenAI system can enable and constrain work depending on organizational enactment

### 2. Algorithmic Control

Kellogg, K. C., Valentine, M. A., & Christin, A. (2020). Algorithms at work: The new contested terrain of control. *Academy of Management Annals, 14*(1), 366-410. https://doi.org/10.5465/annals.2018.0174

- Why it matters: gives the paper a precise control vocabulary rather than generic surveillance language
- Use it for: monitoring intensity, contested control, and discretion-shaping mechanisms

### 3. Workplace Dignity

Lucas, K. (2015). Workplace dignity: Communicating inherent, earned, and remediated dignity. *Journal of Management Studies, 52*(5), 621-646. https://doi.org/10.1111/joms.12133

- Why it matters: gives a clean management and organization reference for dignity as a work construct
- Use it for: recognition, standing, and the relational quality of treatment at work

### 4. Dignity As A Broader Work Concept

Hodson, R. (2001). *Dignity at work*. Cambridge University Press. https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511499333

- Why it matters: canonical book-level anchor for dignity in work relations
- Use it for: framing dignity as more than a narrow psychological state

### 5. Dignity And Well-Being Distinction

Hodson, R. (2001). Worker dignity and well-being. In *Dignity at work* (pp. 237-258). Cambridge University Press. https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511499333.010

- Why it matters: helps separate the paper's focal construct from generic well-being language
- Use it for: explaining why dignity and well-being overlap but are not identical

### 6. Autonomy Distinction

Gagne, M., & Deci, E. L. (2005). Self-determination theory and work motivation. *Journal of Organizational Behavior, 26*(4), 331-362. https://doi.org/10.1002/job.322

- Why it matters: gives a precise autonomy and volition reference
- Use it for: clarifying that autonomy is one component relevant to dignity, not the full outcome

### 7. Human-AI Collaboration In Knowledge Work

Sowa, K., Przegalinska, A., & Ciechanowski, L. (2021). Cobots in knowledge work. *Journal of Business Research, 125*, 135-142. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jbusres.2020.11.038

- Why it matters: directly supports the paper's claim that AI reshapes managerial and professional work rather than merely automating tasks
- Use it for: human-AI collaboration in knowledge-intensive settings

### 8. Generative AI Productivity And Quality Effects

Dell'Acqua, F., McFowland, E., Mollick, E. R., Lifshitz-Assaf, H., Kellogg, K., Rajendran, S., Krayer, L., Candelon, F., & Lakhani, K. R. (2023). Navigating the jagged technological frontier: Field experimental evidence of the effects of AI on knowledge worker productivity and quality. *SSRN Electronic Journal*. https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.4573321

- Why it matters: strongest current empirical anchor for uneven GenAI performance effects in knowledge work
- Use it for: motivating why performance gains alone are an incomplete lens

## Reference Strategy

Use references 1-4 as the conceptual core, 5-6 for construct boundary work, and 7-8 for phenomenon grounding. Do not overload the TREO draft with a long review; use this spine to support only the claims that are central to the mechanism and contribution.
